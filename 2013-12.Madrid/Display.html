Display of Constrained Elements

If you have obtained constrained elements in the previous exercise, you can use these to run this exercise. You can use pre-calculated elements otherwise (see below).

UCSC and Ensembl accept several file formats to upload/display your own data. The most commonly used are BED for simple features and GFF for more complex data (like genes where you would want to define transcripts, exons, CDS, etc). We will use BED files for this exercise.

BED file with GERP elements: [pecan.mfa.rates.bed]

BED file with PhastCons elements: [tba_human.bed]

If you are using your own file, check its content first. You need at least 3 columns with the chromosome name, the start and the end position.

Upload to Ensembl

Uploading your data on Ensembl is done via the "Manage your data" button. Here are some instructions:
  1. Go to the Ensembl main page: http://www.ensembl.org
  2. Click on Human
  3. Click on [  Display your data in Ensembl ]
  4. Fill in the form
    • Give a name to the track. Anything from "Constrained element" to your pet's name will do.
    • Make sure the species is human and the assembly is GRCh37 (the latest one)
    • Choose the file format (BED in this case)
    • Upload your constrained elements file.
    • Click on [Upload].
  5. You should get a page like: 
    
Thank you. Your file uploaded successfully

File uploaded: pecan.mfa.rates.bed (BED file, Homo sapiens)

Total features found: 55

Go to first region with data: 13:32886745-32986745
  6. Click on the link provided and look at your data. You can compare them to others available constrained elements, genomic features like exons, etc. You can also compare GERP and PhastCons elements if you have uploaded both files.
  7. You can go back to "Manage your data" to store or share your data.

Upload to UCSC

Uploading your data on UCSC is done via the "" button. Here are some instructions:
  1. Go to the UCSC main page: http://genome.ucsc.edu/
  2. Click on "Genome Browser" (top-left).
  3. Make sure the species is human and the assembly is GRCh37.
  4. Click on the "add custom tracks" button.
  5. Check the species/assembly info again.
  6. Click on "Browse..." and "Submit" your constrained elements file.
  7. You will get a Results page with a table similar to this:
    Name Description Type Doc Items Pos
    User TrackUser Supplied Trackbed 28chr13:
  8. Click on "go to genome browser" button.
  9. Adjust the view by zooming out and moving the view.
  10. You can also click on the right margin of the track to change the display or choose among the options in the lower part of the alignments.
  11. The URL can be shared.
